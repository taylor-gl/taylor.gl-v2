# Some highlights from other Halfhaven writers (since the last digest)
- [roundness of numbers is complicated](https://aprilsr.substack.com/p/roundness-of-numbers-is-complicated) (April) --- Contra Inkhaven resident Signore Galilei, April of Apriiori cleanly argues essentially that you can't describe what we mean by the "roundness" of a number with a formula, because e.g. 25 is rounder than 30 when dealing with cents, but not when dealing with seconds.
- [We write numbers backward](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eS5F8_BuVFg) (lsusr) --- I started this fun video thinking, "no we don't", and ended it thinking "we totally write numbers backward!"
- [E-Prime](https://tiled-with-pentagons.blogspot.com/2025/11/43-e-prime.html) (Lorxus) --- An overview of an interesting, restricted form of English with the worst, cringiest name ever. I definitely **be** a "to be" user and have no plans of paring back my usage of the King's verb, but I appreciate the thoughts about what kinds of language can be unclear.
- [Husky Syndrome](https://lifeimprovementschemes.substack.com/p/husky-syndrome) (Aaron) --- On the mindset of social anxiety with a brilliant analogy to sled-pulling dogs.
- [Supervillain Monologues are Unrealistic](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/azzEWbNncapwNzngx/supervillain-monologues-are-unrealistic) (Algon) --- Real-life villains monologue endlessly about what they plan to do, and nobody listens. Startup founders, on the other hand, are anxious to tell people their master plans, for fear someone will think they're foolish (or steal their idea). But nobody will listen anyway, so feel free to monologue as much as you'd like.
- [The Mortifying Ordeal of Knowing Thyself] (Philipreal) --- Contrary to the grandiose title, it's a relatable blog post about nervousness when posting Halfhaven blog posts, and a desire to do less than your best so nobody can judge your true best. It suggests a strategy of posting the occasional higher-effort post, which I have been following myself (two out of my last five were higher-effort).
- [I Admit, I Am Ignorant of Many Things](https://keltan.substack.com/p/i-admit-i-am-ignorant-of-many-things) (keltan) --- An ode to saying "I don't know".
